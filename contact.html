Our great guide Douglas Nagi

Doug (or Delta November) holds the KPSGA Gold Level Guiding Award and has endeavoured numerous research projects on ethno-botany and ethnography in the Northern Regions of Kenya. Doug underwent gun-handling with the British Army in Nanyuki, and is certified in Astronomy after studying under a visiting Oxford professor. In his free time you will find Doug giving lectures about the medicinal uses of plants at Nairobi’s National Museum. Additional to guiding, Doug is the Conservation, Culture and Community Manager at Cottar’s 1920s Camp and Bush Villa.

Land Rover trouble

Out on our second day of safari, we ended up getting the truck stuck in a huge hole that was created due the heavy rains they had the previous weeks.Thank god for Douglas and his Maasai guide "superstar" for getting some rocks and wood to pry the truck out. Pretty sure Douglas still has a scar on his hand from the tire jack. Tracy and I used that time to view some hippos that were in the river just a 100 yards away.

Tuskers beer and chips

Have to admit this was my favorite part of the day, just relaxing in the open plains watching the widlife go by and having a few Tuskers beers with some home made chips that were very addictive. I could have spent 15 hours a day out there looking at the elephants. By far one of the most exilarating trips we have ever taken.
